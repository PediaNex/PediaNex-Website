Running a Vaccination Camp or School Screening? A Practical Checklist

Published July 22, 2026 · PediaNex Team

A camp or school screening is a different problem from a regular clinic day: no fixed desk, no reliable Wi-Fi, a queue of children who aren't existing patients, and a hard stop time. This is the checklist we'd hand a clinic team before they load the car.

Running a vaccination camp or school screening well comes down to three things: preparing supplies and paperwork before you leave the clinic, capturing readings fast and offline while on site, and having a way to get a takeaway into a parent's or teacher's hands the same day.

Before the camp

Supplies and logistics

  • Vaccine cold chain: confirm the vaccine carrier, ice packs, and a thermometer are packed and checked, not assumed to already be in the vehicle.
  • Measuring equipment: a working scale, height/length board, and measuring tape, calibrated before you leave — not discovered broken on site.
  • Consent forms: printed or digital consent ready for the school or camp organizer to distribute in advance, so you're not collecting signatures in the queue.
  • Power and connectivity: a charged power bank or laptop battery, and a plan that assumes the venue's Wi-Fi will be unreliable or absent.
  • Printer: a portable printer or pre-printed takeaway slips, if a same-day handout is part of the plan.

Staffing and roles

  • Assign one person to registration/entry, one to measurements, and one to the clinician's station — a single person trying to do all three creates the queue's real bottleneck.
  • Decide in advance how a child without an existing patient record will be handled — this is usually most of the queue at a school screening.

During the camp

On-site workflow

  • Capture height, weight, and any other vitals directly at the measuring station, rather than on a separate paper log to be transcribed later — transcription is where errors creep in.
  • Flag any reading that looks like it needs follow-up (well outside an expected growth range, a missed vaccine dose, a concerning vital) at the point of capture, not after the whole camp is done.
  • Keep a simple running count of children seen against the expected list — schools and camp organizers will ask before you leave the venue.
The core problem this solves for: a full clinic patient record — history, past visits, existing prescriptions — usually doesn't exist yet for a child at a school screening, and creating one on the spot for every child in a queue isn't realistic in the time available. What's needed instead is a lightweight way to capture vitals and a growth chart for a child who may not be in the system at all, without that data getting lost the moment you leave the venue.

This is exactly what a standalone camp mode is built to avoid re-solving every time.

How PediaNex handles this

PediaNex includes a standalone outreach and camp screening mode that records a child's vitals and a growth chart without needing to open or create a full patient record first. Readings are saved locally on the device and can be printed on the spot, so a camp team isn't dependent on a live internet connection to get a takeaway into a parent's or teacher's hands before the child leaves. After the camp

Follow-up

  • Sync or export the day's records as soon as you're back on a reliable connection, rather than leaving it for "later in the week."
  • Route flagged cases into a proper follow-up — a call to the parent, or a note for the child's next regular visit if they're already a patient.
  • Share a simple summary back with the school or camp organizer — how many children were seen, and how many were flagged for follow-up — while it's still fresh.

Frequently asked questions

What is an outreach or camp screening mode in a pediatric EMR?

A standalone mode that records a child's vitals and a growth chart on the spot, without needing to open or create a full patient record, then saves the data locally and can print it immediately.

Do you need internet access to run a vaccination camp?

Not if the tool you're using is built for it. A camp-mode workflow that saves locally on the device can capture readings without a live connection and sync or export later.

What should be printed on the spot at a school screening?

A simple takeaway with the child's recorded vitals and growth chart position, so the child or a teacher has something physical to hand to a parent the same day.
